TV Turns On but No Picture? Here’s What to Check

Hook: Screen is black but the sound works — scary, right? Before you panic and think the TV is dead, try these simple checks. Most times the fix is quick and doesn’t cost much.

Quick Answer

The most common reasons are wrong input/source, loose cables, backlight failure, faulty HDMI/AV ports, or a software/firmware glitch. Start with input and cables; then move to hardware and software checks.

Causes & Fixes (Step-by-step)
  1. Wrong input / Source selected
    Why: TV is on but set to an unused HDMI/AV channel.
    Fix: Press the TV remote’s Source/Input button and cycle through inputs (HDMI1, HDMI2, AV, TV). Try the channel where your device (set-top box, DVD, console) is connected.
  2. Loose or damaged cables
    Why: HDMI, AV, or power cables can be loose or faulty.
    Fix: Turn off the TV and device; unplug and replug HDMI/AV cables firmly. If possible, test with another HDMI cable or another HDMI port.
  3. Backlight failure (screen very dark)
    Why: TV image is present but backlight is dead — sound works but display is black.
    Fix: In a dark room, shine a torch closely at the screen when the TV is on. If you faintly see the picture, the backlight/LED strips are faulty and need professional repair.
  4. Loose internal connections / power board issue
    Why: Power board or internal connectors may be failing.
    Fix: This requires a technician — avoid DIY unless you know electronics. Ask a pro to check the power/inverter board.
  5. Software / Firmware glitch
    Why: Smart TVs sometimes hang on boot or have firmware bugs.
    Fix: Power-cycle the TV: unplug for 60 seconds, plug back and power on. If available, update the TV firmware via USB or built-in update option.
  6. Faulty external device
    Why: The set-top box, DVD, or console may be at fault (sound routed but video not).
    Fix: Disconnect all inputs; turn on TV with nothing connected. If the TV still shows no picture, the problem is the TV. If a picture appears, reconnect devices one at a time to find the faulty one.
  7. HDMI handshake / HDCP issues
    Why: Some devices and TV models fail to establish HDMI encryption properly.
    Fix: Try a different HDMI cable, swap ports, or temporarily disable HDCP settings on the device if possible.
Quick Safety & Pro Tip

Do not open the TV or attempt internal repairs unless you’re trained. TV capacitors and power components can hold dangerous charge even when unplugged. For backlight, inverter, or power-board issues, call a certified technician.

When to Repair vs Replace
  • Repair if: TV is under 6–7 years old and the fault is a replaceable part (HDMI board, backlight strip, power board) — repair often costs a fraction of a new TV.
  • Consider replacing if: Repair estimate is >50% of a new TV or multiple parts are failing (age 8+ years, panel issues). Modern 4K TVs can be affordable and energy-efficient.
Final Takeaway

Don’t jump to buy a new TV at the first sign of a black screen. Start with the simple checks: input, cables, and devices.
